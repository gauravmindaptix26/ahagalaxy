Introduction

What is Gantry Advertising (Gate Entry Branding)?

Gantry advertising (also called gate entry branding) is an outdoor format placed at entry points—city gates, market gateways, corridor starts and major approach roads. It works because it captures the arrival moment when people naturally look for direction, landmarks and destination cues.

FAQ

FAQs about gantry advertising (gate entry branding)

Quick answers about pricing, entry zones, creative guidelines and execution timelines.

Where does gantry advertising work best? +

It works best at city gates, market entrances, corridor starts and town arrival points where attention naturally resets and people look for destination cues.

How is gantry branding different from a normal billboard? +

Gantry branding owns the gateway moment and often spans wider. Billboards usually work as route recall points after the entry has already happened.

What type of creative works best on gantry formats? +

Short bold headlines, high-contrast visuals and a clean hierarchy perform best—especially when traffic is moving and viewing time is limited.

Can gantry advertising be combined with other OOH formats? +

Yes. Gate entry branding becomes stronger when paired with highway hoardings and city unipoles to build a full-route campaign memory chain.
